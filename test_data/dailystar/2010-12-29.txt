people's perception about service sectors
it is a recognised fact that the service sectors of bangladesh cause hardships and sufferings to the people. it is not that the service sectors in what is now bangladesh did not harass the people during pakistan period or during the british colonial days. but people's sufferings have increased manifold during the last three decades or so. people's perception is that service sector establishments in the government are places where red tape, harassment, bribery and other corrupt practices are common features.

finance minister abul maal abdul muhit has recently come out with an interesting suggestion. he said that, in order to ease the traffic congestion of dhaka, his government was thinking of either banning private cars from the city or making it compulsory for the private cars to carry 4-5 passengers. the idea seemed to me not only unrealistic but also unreasonable.
