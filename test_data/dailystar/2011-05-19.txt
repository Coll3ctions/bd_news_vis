although the bangladeshi microfinance sector is mature and its assets constitute approximately 2% of gdp, the sector is still unfortunately in its infancy when it comes to the use of technology. two major problems, inter alia, arise due to this technological infancy -- it substantially reduces outreach capabilities of the mfis, decelerates the pace of poverty alleviation, and creates a bubble of over-indebtedness or overlapping that posits a major threat for the whole industry. to explain this, we need to understand how mfis operate in bangladesh and what their fundamental requirements for automation are.
